George Orwell

George Orwell was a British novelist, essayist, and journalist celebrated for his profound insights into politics, society, and human nature. Renowned for his timeless classics 1984 and Animal Farm, Orwell explored themes of totalitarianism, propaganda, and the struggle for freedom. His distinctive style combined sharp wit with deep moral purpose, making his works enduringly relevant. Orwell's essays, including "Shooting an Elephant" and "Politics and the English Language", further cemented his legacy as one of the 20th century's most influential writers.
